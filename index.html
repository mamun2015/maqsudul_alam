Dr. Maqsudul Alam
Inventor of Jute Genome

1954-2014

Bangladeshi Scientist Professor Dr Maqsudul Alam is globally famous figure as an inventor of Jute genome. He invented the genome sequence of ‘Tosha’ jute – the Jute Plant Draft Genome – in June, 2010. Subsequently, in 2012 Maqsudul Alam and his cooperative team introduced "Macrophomina phaseolina", a soil borne fungus, which obstructs growing about 500 species of crops including jute. He has been a recognized figure because of his research in sequencing the Deoxyribonucleic (DNA) of the traditional variety of jute. With this invention jute also knon as Golden fibre to become healthy, high yielding, weather-tolerant.

Dr. Maqsudul Alam achieved four milestones in genomics - sequencing the genomes of Papaya, Rubber, Jute and Fungus. Dr. Maqsudul Alam was a professor of the University of Hawaii at Manoa and a member of advisory board at Shahjalal University of Science and Technology, Bangladesh.
